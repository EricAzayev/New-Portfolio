## The LCAVEMAN Mission - Lunar Colonization Preliminary Design Review

**Full Title:** The LCAVEMAN Mission (Lunar Colonization Accessibility Venture for the Evaluation of the Magnetosphere and Ancient Materials)  
**Timeline:** September 2024 - December 2024  
**Team:** Team 25  
**Budget:** $225 Million

### Original Description

Team 25 created the Preliminary Design Review for the LCAVEMAN mission, which aims to explore the Marius Hills Pit on the Moon as a potential site for future lunar habitation. The project aims to conduct the earliest subsurface exploration of the pit, mapping its terrain and evaluating the feasibility of human settlement while falling under a $225 million budget.

As a Program Analyst, I helped the team leads coordinate projects while assessing schedules, changes, risks, and scope. I worked on Gannt Charts, N^2 Charts, budget calculations using tools and best practices outlined by NASA, scope control, bases of estimate that set firm expectations in scoping and budgeting, project management strategies, and change control implementations. Through extensive research in official NASA engineering and project management handbooks and feedback received with every deliverable, I can develop NASA-aligned concepts for extended and complex projects.

### Strategic Objectives

1. **Subsurface Mapping**: Earliest systematic exploration of the Marius Hills Pit terrain
2. **Habitability Assessment**: Evaluate feasibility for sustainable human settlement
3. **Resource Evaluation**: Assess magnetosphere access and ancient materials for scientific study
